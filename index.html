Six worlds, one state

Tilt a card — each valley wears its own light.

Shimla

The old summer capital — Victorian ridges, deodar corridors, and toy-train switchbacks carved for a colonial escape from the plains.

1,969 m

Kullu Valley

The Silver Valley of the Beas — apple orchards, temple towers, and the roar of the river all the way to Manali.

3,800 m

Spiti Valley

A cold desert behind the Himalaya — moonscape cliffs, thousand-year monasteries, and skies with almost no air between you and them.

2,082 m

Dharamshala

Pine ridges below the Dhauladhar wall, home to Tibetan monasteries and the low murmur of prayer wheels.

2,400 m

Bir Billing

The paragliding capital of India — launch from a Himalayan ridge and land in a sea of tea gardens.

2,438 m

Chamba

An isolated hill kingdom of miniature painting, wooden temples, and the Ravi river cutting through the deodars.

devbhoomi

A land governed as much by devtas as by districts

☸

Kullu Dussehra

Over 200 local deities are carried down from their villages in painted palanquins to pay court to Lord Raghunath — a festival that starts where every other Dussehra ends.

✦

Losar

The Tibetan-Buddhist new year, kept closely in Spiti and Lahaul, with masked Cham dances performed in monastery courtyards cut into the cliffs.

⌂

Kath-Kuni architecture

Alternating timber and stone bands built to flex in earthquakes — a construction method older than any building code, still standing after centuries.

✁

Chamba Rumal

Double-satin embroidery so precise the reverse side is identical to the front — a needle-craft once sent as royal gifts between hill states.

for the restless

Elevation is the whole point

Paragliding

Bir Billing hosts a world-class launch site — ride thermals along the Dhauladhar for up to an hour at a time.

High-altitude trekking

Hampta Pass, Pin Parvati, and the Great Himalayan National Park cross glaciers most maps still mark as blank.

Skiing & Winter Sports

Narkanda and Solang Valley turn to powder from December to February, with heli-skiing runs above Manali.

White-water rafting

The Beas at Kullu runs grade II–III rapids in summer, fed entirely by the melt it's named for.
